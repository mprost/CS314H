Ancient Miller Time

ndd@sunbar.mc.duke.edu (Ned Danieley)
(smirk, topical)

On Thursday, Nov 5, the New York Times News Service reported 
that archaeologists have found the earliest chemical evidence
for beer. "It's chemical substantiation of what were archaeological
arguments before," Dr. Patrick E. McGovern, an archaeological
chemist at the University Museum of Archaeology and Anthropology
at the University of Pennsylvania, said in an interview Wednesday.

Asked the meaning of finding both the earliest known wine and beer
in the same room, McGovern replied, "I think a lot of serious
drinking was going on there."

Ned Danieley (ndd@sunbar.mc.duke.edu)
