Particularly selective readers will probably not enjoy the digests, and may want to killfile RHF jokes with "Digest" in the title - ed.

>From: andrewd@nospam.ozemail.com.au (Andrew Davis)
>Subject: Another millennium so soon?

Listening to the Voice of America on shortwave in the early days of January 2000, I heard an announcer remark that the entire subject of "the new millennium" had become quite tiresome, and that he wished the word millennium could be removed from the vocabulary.

He added, "we can reinstate the word when we next need it, in a hundred years."

>From: neden@thetoybox.org (Kevin Neden)
>Subject: Mensa's Y2K Readiness

While I was lined up to purchase my post-Christmas discount calendar, I spotted the "Mensa 365 Day Calendar" for 2000. Admittedly, not all of the other daily calendars for sale said, "366 Day Calendar," but at least you'd expect the smartest people on the planet to know 2000 is a Leap Year.
