One Question Too Many

SARDog3752@aol.com
(smirk)

I remember this from a newspaper article about a seminar on lawyer courtroom
tactics (I think that was the subject).  The speaker was hammering on the
issue of making your point then stopping.  He described a case in which a man
was accused of biting another man's nose off during a fight.  The star
prosecution witness was being cross-examined by the defendant's attorney.

ATTY: Did you see the defendant bite off the victim's nose?
WITNESS: No
ATTY: Then how do you know he bit off the victim's nose?
WITNESS: I saw him spit it out.
