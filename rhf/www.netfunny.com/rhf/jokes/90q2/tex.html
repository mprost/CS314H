      I recently received news from a friend close to, well sort of close
  to, 'Knuth' (must be said with a tone of awe and respect).  As the writer 
  of teX, the slave/guts of LATEx [1], he was asked why he went through 
  all the trouble to re-invent the wheel - after all what about troff ?  

	His reply:

      "Well...  being an old Cobol programmer - I missed having to type 3 
  times the code to get the same results as with traditional software [2]."

[1] - LAtEx can be mispronounced as the non-oil based paint, but it is not 
    as easy to apply.

[2] - even most lAtEx code is a bit \begin{comment} wordy \end{comment}.

	We now know better:

   Real programmers use troff.  Business students use lATEx/CoBOl.
--
   Joe Miller      Michigan State University
       An old troff user who's heard too much ruh ha ha about lAteX.
