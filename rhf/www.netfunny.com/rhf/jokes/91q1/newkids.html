Public Enemy Number Two

loren@cis.ohio-state.edu (Loren Schwiebert)
(topical, chuckle)

The following appeared in the February 8, 1991 issue of The Columbus Dispatch:

Spin magazine surveyed students for its March issue, asking, "If law and normal consequences were suspended for one day, whom would you kill?" Of 750 students polled, more than half either left the question blank or responded that killing is entirely unacceptable. Saddam Hussein was the obvious and overwhelming favorite as murder target, followed by New Kids on the Block.
