Incredible advances in RAM size reduction!

swalton@epas.utoronto.ca (Steven Ashton Walton)
(smirk, computers, work)

In a recent departmental newsletter, the following notice appeared:

> NEW COMPUTER EQUIPMENT:
>      Within the next couple of weeks, two new 386 SX-40 (120 mg
> hard drive, 2 floppy drives - 5 1/4" and 3 1/2") will be
> delivered.  One will be placed in the student study room
> replacing one of the old machines there.  The other will take the
> place of the dying unit where the laserprinter is located.  You
> no doubt will be pleased to learn that the HP II will be replaced
> by the HP LaserJet 4 (which includes an extra 2 mg of memory).

The advances in memory storage simply amazed me!  I mean, it is hard
to imagine a 120 milligram hard drive holding so much, and that the
laserwriter could be so much faster with the addition of only 2
milligrams of extra RAM.  Wow!
