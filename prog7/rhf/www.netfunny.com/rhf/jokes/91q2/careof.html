One-party election

dana@are.berkeley.edu (Dana E. Keil)
Department of Agricultural and Resource Economics, UC Berkeley
(chuckle)

Brought back from the Sudan when it was a one-party dictatorship.

A young man, going to "vote" for the first time in a national election, got nervous in the voting booth and mistakenly voted against the ruling party. When he told his father, the old man ran to the polling place to try to correct the error that would probably spell doom for the whole family. "Please, you must save us," he told the election officials in a panic, "can the error be changed?"

"Don't worry," they replied, "it's already been taken care of."
