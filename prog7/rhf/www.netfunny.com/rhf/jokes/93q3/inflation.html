Think about the US Dollar...

paul@connect.com.au (Paul Davis)
Accademia degli Incamminati
(topical, smirk, economics)

From an international news report (20 July 93):

	U.S. Cleveland Federal Reserve President, Jerry 
	Jordan, said "we have not yet reached an 
	environment where households believe that the rate 
	of inflation in the future will be lower than it is 
	today."  He went on to say "we would like people to 
	think about the U.S. dollar in the same way as they 
	think about the number of feet in a mile or ounces 
	in a pound."

How is that?  As an archaic English unit of measurement not
widely understood and no longer in use internationally?
