Health Profile: Mike Tyson

Wednesday, January 30, 2002 - Toronto, Ontario


What's wrong with Mike Tyson?

With everything going for him, why is he such an idiot?

Well, after a tremendous amount of research, I think I have an answer for you. I have concluded that Mike Tyson is suffering from several different very rare mental illnesses, as described briefly below:

  1. Paranoid Dillusional Minimillia (PDM): In short, Tyson has no idea how large and strong he is. Probably the squeaky voice he hears from his mouth misleads him into believing he is weak and the rest of the world is out to get him. No, Mike, if you were weak, the rest of the world would have had you a long time ago.
  2. Earkle's Disorder (ED): This rare disorder effects a small minority of black men who appear on television. While they have the coolness potential inherent in being black, and on television, he shows signs of the two main symptoms of ED: he's not cool, and he talks with a squeaky voice.
  3. Canabalism: Tyson's rare form of canabilism involves eating thighs and ears of various victims. This dates back to his childhood when he was probably bitten by an animal, which emotionally scarred him, especially since he took action against the animal by eating it alive. Now, when Tyson is faced with a challenge, he puts his money where his mouth is.
