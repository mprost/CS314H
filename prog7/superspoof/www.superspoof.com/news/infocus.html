In Focus ::

July, 2004

In his recent movie, Farenheit 9/11, Michael Moore all but declares that George Bush is an idiot, especially when it comes to foreign affairs. While we do not necessarily disagree, we wanted to probe further to find out more about Mr. Moore's own savvy in this area.

Let's review.

It seems Michael Moore is under the impression that people from Toronto don't lock their doors.

Michael Moore thinks it is appropriate to go in to foreign countries, like Canada, and thwart election laws to promote his movies.

Michael Moore thinks Canada's health care system is simply amazing and does not need fixing. I will seriously consider his point during the 10 months I have on my waiting list for a gastro-intestinal diagnostic procedure.

Michael Moore is capable of successfully pointing out Canada on a map.

Well, heck, that's one for four. Perhaps Michael Moore is pretty smart about other countries after all.

But, just like Michael Moore "documentaries", we're not trying to influence you -- you decide for yourself.

May, 2002

With the non-starter talk of merger between the Canadian Alliance and the Progressive Conservative Party, our in-depth investigative report looks at the coming realignment on the left.

With the breakup of the NDP nearly inevitable, several prominent Liberal backbenchers have formed the BHLC, or the Bleeding Heart Liberal Coalition.

Speculation in Ottawa is that this group may join the non-Labour split of the NDP, factions now bored with the Green Party, the rump of the Communist Party, and pro-environment Marxist-Leninist Party supporters. (A source told the BCB that the Marxist-Leninist Party of Canada recently split into 27 separate parties due to various differences of opinion. Of particular contention was whether the upcoming "new socialist pro-persons revolution" should include a special ceremony hanging all corporate CEOs.)

We look at whether or not this new left-wing coalition will finally offer Canadians a true alternative strong enough to topple the Liberal majority.
