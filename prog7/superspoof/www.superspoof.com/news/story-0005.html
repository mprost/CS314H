Gordon Campbell is Bad
Monday, January 27, 2002 - Victoria, British Columbia

British Columbia Teachers, Health Workers, Bureaucrats, Courthouse Workers, Police Dogs, NDP MLA's, Labour Leaders, and three guys all named Robert agree on one thing: Gordon Campbell is bad.

Gordon Campbell, British Columbia's newish Premier has introduced a program of sweeping structural reforms throughout all manner of things which involve the Provincial Government.

Angry crowds have formed outside the Legislature on separate occasions in the last few weeks since the announcement. On one occasion, three guys named Robert were chanting "Campbell, Campbell, You are Mean. We want our Government Fat, not Lean." On another occasion, larger crowds chanted, "Campbell, Campbell your not nice, the way you chop and grind and slice."

"Change is bad," said Ted Smith of Local 3712 on Friday, "I just wish he'd stop being mean and nasty and leave us alone. We deserve to make $37.50 an hour while everyone else is starving on a $7.00 minimum wage. It's our way of standing up for the working people of British Columbia."

"Union people smell funny," said a source from within the Government on the condition of anonymity, "while we believe our structural reforms will be good over the long term for the people of British Columbia, we are mainly doing this because we hate union people and all their stupid little chants. Solidarity Forever my ass."

A special weekend session of the Legislature was held to pass special legislation to force a teachers contract, modify severance provisions for Government employees, and to pass an "equity" resolution designed "to offend and anger anyone not specifically and directly affected by the current and upcoming actions of the Provincial Government." This final resolution was passed unanimously, including support from the NDP opposition.

"We know alot of people are upset by these measures," our inside source said, "so we didn't want ordinary British Columbians to feel we didn't also want to offend them. We want to make sure everyone feels equally angry."

A province-wide teacher's walkout is planned for today.
